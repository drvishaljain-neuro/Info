Synapse Symphony Inc.

Translating advanced neuromodulation research into clinically deployable, patient-centered therapies.

Visit synapsesymphony.com

As Co-Founder and Chief Operating Officer, I lead the translation of non-invasive and minimally invasive brain stimulation from research prototypes to regulated medical products. My work spans technology strategy, regulatory planning, clinical translation, and commercialization.

Our Vision & Impact

Synapse Symphony is building a precision neuromodulation platform to provide safer, more accessible alternatives to traditional deep brain stimulation and chronic pain pharmacotherapy. By combining high-density stimulation, intelligent current steering, and data-driven personalization, we aim to:

  • Deliver clinically meaningful pain relief and functional improvement without invasive implants.
  • Enable clinicians to configure and personalize therapy at the level of networks, not just single targets.
  • Reduce barriers to access by designing systems suitable for both clinic and at-home use.

Entrepreneurship & Industry Leadership

As Co-Founder and COO of Synapse Symphony Inc., I work at the interface of engineering, clinical practice, and business. My focus is to convert high-fidelity neuromodulation research into products that can be evaluated by clinicians, reimbursed by payers, and adopted at scale.

Building Synapse Symphony Inc.

Synapse Symphony grew out of collaborative work at Carnegie Mellon University on ultra-high-density transcranial and minimally invasive stimulation. I have been involved from inception in defining the company’s clinical focus, technology roadmap, and commercialization strategy, with an emphasis on chronic pain and functional recovery.

My Role as COO

  • Commercial Strategy: Defining product offerings, clinical use-cases, pricing, and positioning across pain clinics, rehabilitation centers, and health systems.
  • Regulatory Leadership: Coordinating FDA strategy (Q-Sub/Pre-Sub, De Novo vs 510(k)), aligning development with regulatory expectations, and working with external consultants.
  • Clinical Translation: Designing early feasibility studies, pilot protocols, and site engagement for chronic pain and neuro-rehabilitation indications.
  • Technical Integration: Connecting hardware, software, and stimulation algorithms into a unified product, with clear requirements for safety, usability, and manufacturability.
  • Investor & Partner Engagement: Leading technical narratives, pitch decks, diligence responses, and strategic discussions with payers, clinical partners, and industry collaborators.
  • Operational Execution: Managing timelines, budgets, grant milestones, vendor relationships, and documentation to keep research, engineering, and regulatory activities aligned.

Key Milestones

  • DARPA Embedded Entrepreneur Initiative award, supporting commercialization strategy and regulatory de-risking.
  • Participation in clinically focused accelerators such as AlphaLab Health, with structured guidance on go-to-market, reimbursement, and clinical validation.
  • Development of staged product roadmap: software-driven personalization layer leveraging existing FDA-cleared hardware, followed by integrated neuromodulation hardware.
  • Structured interviews with clinicians, health system stakeholders, and payer representatives to refine indications, workflows, and value propositions.
  • Ongoing work on IP strategy and licensing with Carnegie Mellon University to support long-term defensibility.

Broader Industry & Commercialization Experience

Beyond Synapse Symphony, my work frequently involves bridging academic research with translational and industry requirements. This includes:

  • Co-developing proposals and studies that align clinical endpoints, technical feasibility, and payer expectations.
  • Collaborating with payers and consultants to understand coverage pathways, health-economic considerations, and evidence requirements.
  • Supporting IP generation, provisional patent drafting, and licensing strategies for neurotechnology platforms.
  • Advising on integration of neuromodulation systems into existing clinical workflows and infrastructure.

Forward Focus

The long-term goal is to establish a robust pipeline for translating neuromodulation research into regulated, clinically impactful products. This requires simultaneous attention to engineering rigor, biological insight, patient experience, and system-level constraints—a combination that shapes both my scientific and entrepreneurial work.
