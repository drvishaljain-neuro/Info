Translational Neurotechnology & Neuromodulation

I lead a multidisciplinary incubator space focused on developing and validating the next generation of neurotechnologies that bridge fundamental discovery and real-world application. Within the Carnegie Mellon ecosystem and through close collaborations with Prof. Maysam Chamanzar and Prof. Pulkit Grover, my research program integrates neuroscience, engineering, and clinical insight to create tools that advance how we sense, stimulate, and interface with the brain and body.

Our mission is to translate cutting-edge research into practical, impactful neurotechnologies from minimally invasive brain-stimulation systems and high-density neural interfaces to innovative optical imaging and sensing platforms. By combining rigorous scientific methods with a translational mindset, this work aims to deliver technologies that address unmet needs in neuroscience, neurology, pain medicine, and human health.
